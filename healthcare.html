Healthcare Guide

What to carry for hospital visits, how to prepare, and what to do in common situations. Clear and practical.

Hospital Visit Checklist

Tip

Most delays come from missing documents or unclear information. Prepare early and carry backups.

  • ✓
    Identification. ID card or any accepted identification.
  • ✓
    Medical history. Notes, previous prescriptions, or lab results if you have them.
  • ✓
    Emergency contacts. Phone numbers of family/guardian.
  • ✓
    Payment plan. Know your budget and what you can handle if top-ups are needed.

If you’re visiting a hospital:

Steps you should follow

  1. Describe symptoms clearly

    Keep it short and accurate: what, when, and how severe.

  2. Ask for clarification

    If you don’t understand a test or medication, ask.

  3. Keep copies

    Store prescriptions and results, even as photos on your phone.

  4. Follow instructions

    Take medicine as directed. Don’t freestyle doses.

FAQ

What if I don’t have prior medical records?

Explain what you remember: previous conditions, allergies, and past medication reactions.

How do I avoid repeated payments for the same tests?

Keep results and show them during your next visit to avoid unnecessary repetition.

What if I’m sent to buy medicine outside?

Confirm the exact name/dosage, and keep the receipt. Ask if alternatives exist.
